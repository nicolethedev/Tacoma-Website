Why We Love Tacoma

Full of History

Tacoma was founded in the mid-1800s and grew rapidly as a key transportation hub. Its location along Puget Sound made it an ideal port for trade, and in 1873, it became the western terminus of the Northern Pacific Railway, earning the nickname "City of Destiny."

Plenty of Entertainment

Art lovers can explore the city's vibrant museum scene, including the Museum of Glass and the LeMay Car Museum. Live theater and music are abundant, with venues like the Pantages Theater and the Tacoma Dome hosting concerts, Broadway shows, and local performances.

Learn More

Surrounded by Nature

Tacoma is surrounded by diverse and stunning natural landscapes. To the west, Puget Sound offers coastal beauty with opportunities for kayaking, boating, and waterfront walks. Just south of the city, the towering Mount Rainier provides a dramatic backdrop.
