Why We Love Tacoma

Full of History

Tacoma was founded in the mid-1800s and grew rapidly as a key transportation hub. Its location along Puget Sound made it an ideal port for trade, and in 1873, it became the western terminus of the Northern Pacific Railway, earning the nickname "City of Destiny." The city's economy flourished with industries like timber, shipping, and manufacturing. However, Tacoma faced challenges in the 20th century, including economic downturns and industrial decline. In recent decades, the city has undergone revitalization, embracing its arts, culture, and history.

Plenty of Entertainment

Art lovers can explore the city's vibrant museum scene, including the Museum of Glass and the LeMay Car Museum. For outdoor enthusiasts, Point Defiance Park and the Ruston Waterfront offer scenic trails, beaches, and wildlife. Live theater and music are abundant, with venues like the Pantages Theater and the Tacoma Dome hosting concerts, Broadway shows, and local performances. Sports fans can enjoy a Tacoma Rainiers baseball game, while the city's historic districts, trendy shops, and restaurants provide a mix of cultural and culinary experiences.

Surrounded by Nature

Tacoma is surrounded by diverse and stunning natural landscapes. To the west, Puget Sound offers coastal beauty with opportunities for kayaking, boating, and waterfront walks. Just south of the city, the towering Mount Rainier provides a dramatic backdrop, with its national park offering hiking, wildflower meadows, and glacier-fed rivers. Point Defiance Park, one of the largest urban parks in the U.S., features lush forests, scenic viewpoints, and beaches.
